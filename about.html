Our Philosophy

Art and flowers.
The same discipline.

We come from the belief that floral design is a visual art — no different in rigour from fashion, interior design, or fine art. The stems are our medium. The dome, our frame. The arrangement, our composition.

We don't arrange flowers by formula. We compose them by emotion — by the specific feeling a person wants to give, or receive, on a specific day. That is the Tableau way.

What We Believe
I
Everything is intentional

Every stem, every colour, every proportion is a deliberate choice. There are no fillers. There is no excess. Only what belongs.

II
Named, not numbered

Our arrangements are not products — they are compositions. Each one is given a name because a name gives it meaning, memory, and permanence.

III
Luxury is attention

Luxury is not price. Luxury is the time taken to get something exactly right — to not settle for almost beautiful when truly beautiful is possible.

IV
Flowers carry feeling

We believe in the emotional weight of a well-made arrangement. The right flowers, given at the right moment, say what no words can.

Our Journey

Built slowly.
With great care.

The Beginning
Tableau is Founded

What began as a deep obsession with beautiful flowers became an atelier. The Tableau Classic Dome — our first and most loved arrangement — was the piece that started everything. A dome of garden roses, made with the belief that simplicity, when done perfectly, is the highest luxury.

The Series
The Collections Take Shape

The Signature, Ice Cream, and Garden series were born from three distinct feelings — structure, softness, and abundance. Each series became its own world, attracting a different kind of person, a different kind of occasion.

The Growth
Gifting at Scale

Corporate gifting and volume orders became part of the atelier's identity. We proved that luxury can exist at scale — that 100 arrangements made with the same care as one is not only possible, but necessary.

Now
Tableau Today

We are Kuala Lumpur's luxury floral atelier — a creative house rather than a flower shop. Every arrangement that leaves us is a piece of work we're proud of. That has not changed, and will not.
